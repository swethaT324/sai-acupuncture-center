About Acupuncture

Balance the body. Support healing. Restore comfort.

Acupuncture is a holistic healing technique that uses very fine needles at specific points on the body to influence the flow of energy, support recovery, and reduce pain.

What acupuncture is

Acupuncture is a healing technique in which practitioners insert fine needles at points located on meridians. These meridians are understood as channels that carry Qi (Chi), the vital life energy. When this flow becomes imbalanced, pain, discomfort, and illness may appear.

Where it comes from

This practice originated from China and has been used for at least 2,500 years in different styles and forms. According to the World Health Organization, acupuncture is used in many countries around the world, showing its wide acceptance and relevance.

Five elements concept

Traditional China medicine explains health through balance among the five elements: Fire, Earth, Metal, Water, Wood. Each element is linked to specific functions within the body. When aligned properly, the body can feel more balanced, calm, and healthy.

How it helps

Acupuncture aims to optimize energy flow, restore harmony, and reduce health risks. By adjusting imbalances in Qi, it may help support pain relief, relaxation, better circulation, and the body’s own healing response.

Procedure

A clear and comfortable Healing process

The Healing experience is explained step by step so patients know what to expect before treatment begins.

1. Consultation

The Healer listens to the patient’s symptoms, health history, lifestyle, and current concerns.

2. Assessment

The condition is assessed to understand where imbalance, pain, or discomfort may be affecting the body.

3. Needle placement

Very fine sterile needles are gently placed at selected acupuncture points by a trained practitioner.

4. Therapy session

The patient rests while the treatment supports relaxation, balance, and natural healing responses.

5. Follow-up

Depending on the condition, the Healer may recommend repeat visits or additional care guidance.
